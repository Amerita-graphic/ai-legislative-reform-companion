3. Document Analysis Workflow (Relay.app)

This automation runs when a legislative document is submitted. It processes the document through four AI steps and delivers a complete analysis package by email.

Workflow Steps

  1. Manual Trigger — Workflow is started with the document text as input
  2. Generate Legislative Brief — AI produces an executive summary, key provisions, enforcement gaps, and recommended amendments
  3. Generate Cross‑Walk — AI maps evidence to clauses to proposed amendments in a structured table
  4. Extract Numeric Data — AI extracts all penalties, fees, thresholds, and timeframes into a dataset
  5. Generate Visual Explainer — AI produces Canva-ready infographic design instructions
  6. Email Delivery — All four outputs delivered to inbox automatically

Sample Output — Legislative Brief

=== LEGISLATIVE BRIEF === This Act establishes basic tobacco controls including smoke-free public places, health warnings on packs, a ban on sales to children, inspection powers, and some advertising restrictions. However, the rules are limited and the penalties are low, which may reduce deterrence and make compliance uneven. Several provisions also leave loopholes, especially around advertising and packaging warnings. ENFORCEMENT GAPS The packaging warning requirement is likely inadequate — covering only 30% of the front panel with no mention of pictorial warnings, plain packaging, or back-of-pack requirements. The advertising ban is incomplete, leaving loopholes for sponsorship, digital media, and point-of-sale promotion. Penalties are relatively low and may not deter large corporate operators. RECOMMENDED AMENDMENTS • Increase pack warnings to at least 75% front and back with graphic images • Replace partial advertising restrictions with a comprehensive ban across all media • Raise penalties substantially with escalating fines for repeat offences • Add explicit enforcement powers including seizure, compliance notices, and prosecution support • Introduce retailer licensing with suspension powers for repeated violations

4. Weekly Pacific Health Policy Intelligence

Every Monday at 8:00 AM Fiji time, this workflow automatically generates a Pacific health policy intelligence brief and delivers it by email. It covers policy developments across Fiji, Samoa, Tonga, Vanuatu, PNG, Solomon Islands, and regional bodies including SPC, WHO, UNICEF, DFAT, and MFAT.

Workflow Steps

  1. Scheduled Trigger — Runs automatically every Monday at 8:00 AM FJT
  2. Generate Intelligence Brief — AI produces a structured weekly brief covering headline events, country updates, policy stage tracker, key actors, and legislative implications
  3. Email Delivery — Brief delivered to inbox automatically
